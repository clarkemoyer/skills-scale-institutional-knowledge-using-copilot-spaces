# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Facilitates agile ceremonies, supports continuous improvement, coaches the team on agile practices, and removes blockers to enable efficient delivery.

### Responsibilities
- Lead sprint planning, daily standups, and retrospectives
- Guide the team's adherence to agile best practices
- Track process health and suggest improvements
- Collaborate with Project Manager and Developers to escalate and resolve blockers
- Foster psychological safety and team collaboration
- Ensure the team follows the Definition of Done

### Goals
- Optimize team velocity and flow
- Reduce impediments and delays
- Build a culture of continuous improvement
- Maintain healthy team dynamics

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Regular check-ins with Project Manager on delivery status
- Process improvement discussions with the team
- Escalation notes for persistent blockers

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Collaborates on delivery planning, timeline adjustments, and escalation of blockers
- **Product Manager**: Partners on priority adjustments and roadmap changes based on team capacity
- **Developers**: Supports daily execution, removes blockers, and facilitates collaboration
- **QA Lead**: Coordinates on test planning and quality improvements

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
Ensures product usability, designs user flows and interfaces, collects user feedback, and advocates for exceptional user experience throughout the development lifecycle.

### Responsibilities
- Create UI/UX designs, wireframes, and prototypes
- Validate designs with user research and feedback
- Document and communicate usability standards and patterns
- Collaborate with Product Managers, Developers, and stakeholders for optimal user experience
- Conduct usability testing and iterate on designs
- Maintain design system and component library

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce friction in user workflows
- Ensure consistency across product interfaces
- Validate designs with real user data

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and recommendations
- Wireframes and prototypes in design tools
- Collaboration in GitHub Issues for feature requirements

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Partners on feature requirements, user needs, and business goals
- **Developers**: Collaborates on technical feasibility and implementation details
- **Stakeholders**: Aligns on business objectives and usability standards
- **QA Lead**: Works together on acceptance criteria and usability testing

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Transforms business needs into actionable specifications, maintains clear documentation, clarifies requirements, and manages dependencies between business and technical teams.

### Responsibilities
- Analyze business objectives and translate them into user stories and requirements
- Document acceptance criteria, edge cases, and dependencies
- Ensure alignment between engineering deliverables and business objectives
- Review success metrics and KPIs with Product Manager
- Facilitate requirements gathering sessions with stakeholders
- Maintain traceability between business needs and technical implementation

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are clear, complete, and testable
- Minimize rework due to unclear specifications
- Bridge communication gaps between business and technical teams
- Drive data-informed decision making

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user stories
- Acceptance criteria and test scenarios
- Business process documentation
- Stakeholder interview summaries and insights

### Interaction Points
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on feature documentation and success metrics
- **Developers**: Partners on requirements clarification and technical constraints
- **Stakeholders**: Communicates business needs and validates solutions
- **QA Lead**: Works together on test scenarios and acceptance criteria

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
Ensures overall quality of deliverables, manages test planning and execution, coordinates bug triage processes, and drives release acceptance through comprehensive quality assurance.

### Responsibilities
- Define and manage test strategy across the project
- Coordinate automated and manual testing efforts
- Lead bug triage sessions and prioritize defect resolution
- Track quality metrics (test coverage, defect rates, etc.)
- Drive continuous improvements in testing processes
- Ensure release readiness through comprehensive testing
- Maintain test documentation and test case repositories

### Goals
- Deliver high-quality, reliable software
- Minimize production defects
- Optimize test coverage and efficiency
- Reduce time to detect and resolve issues

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case documentation
- Bug reports and quality metrics dashboards
- Release readiness assessments
- Quality retrospectives and improvement proposals

### Interaction Points
- **Developers**: Works closely on test coverage, automated testing, and defect resolution
- **Project Manager**: Partners on release planning and quality gates
- **Product Manager**: Aligns on acceptance criteria and feature quality expectations
- **Scrum Master**: Collaborates on process improvements and blockers related to quality

---

## Stakeholder (Expanded)

### Role Summary
Represents different business functions (e.g., Sales Lead, Support Lead, Legal Advisor, Security Team), provides strategic input, approves project milestones and deliverables, and escalates critical risks. Stakeholders bring targeted perspectives and decision-making authority to ensure projects align with broader business goals.

### Responsibilities
- Provide requirements, constraints, and feedback from their functional area
- Approve key milestones, deliverables, and go/no-go decisions
- Escalate and help resolve critical challenges
- Represent business priorities and communicate impact to their teams
- Participate in project reviews and steering committees
- Ensure compliance with functional standards (legal, security, etc.)

### Goals
- Ensure project outcomes align with business strategy
- Protect interests of their functional area
- Enable informed decision-making at key gates
- Minimize business risk and maximize value

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and approval meetings
- Status updates via email or project dashboards
- Requirements and feedback during planning sessions
- Escalation communications for critical issues

### Interaction Points
- **Project Manager**: Primary contact for status updates, risk escalation, and milestone approvals
- **Product Manager**: Collaborates on priorities, success metrics, and business value
- **Developers**: Provides input on technical constraints and priorities
- **QA Lead**: Reviews quality standards and release readiness
- **UX Designer**: Gives feedback on customer needs and usability requirements

### Examples of Stakeholder Functions
- **Sales Lead**: Represents customer-facing requirements, competitive positioning, and revenue impact
- **Support Lead**: Provides insights on customer pain points, support burden, and documentation needs
- **Legal Advisor**: Ensures compliance with regulations, contracts, and legal standards
- **Security Team**: Validates security controls, threat models, and vulnerability management
- **Finance**: Reviews budget, resource allocation, and ROI projections
- **Operations**: Assesses operational readiness, infrastructure needs, and monitoring requirements
